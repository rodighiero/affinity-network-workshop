A similarity graph of the publications of Dario Rodighiero, computed offline from the title and abstract of every paper. Source and pipeline on GitHub.

Each node is one publication, placed near the others it most resembles. Similarity is computed offline from the title and abstract of every paper.

How the network is built

  1. The Markdown files in _publications/ are parsed and stripped of footnotes, headings, links, code, and bibliographies.
  2. Italian and French abstracts are machine-translated to English with Helsinki-NLP/opus-mt-{it,fr}-en and cached on disk.
  3. Each cleaned title + abstract is embedded as a 768-dimensional vector by the BAAI/bge-base-en-v1.5 model.
  4. Pairwise cosine similarity yields a dense matrix over every pair of papers.
  5. An edge is drawn from each paper to its single strongest neighbour, unless even that best score falls below 0.70, in which case the paper stays unconnected.
  6. A D3 force simulation lays out the graph; the arrangement is pre-computed offline so it loads already settled.
  7. A paper that is a translation of another is joined only to its original, by a forced edge drawn dashed, rather than linked by similarity.
